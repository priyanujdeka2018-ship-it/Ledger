What This Role Really Is
Stripping away the Google-speak

The Plain-English Summary

Google's advertising support arm — gTech Ads — provides troubleshooting and technical support to advertisers worldwide. Much of this support is delivered through scaled vendor operations: outsourced partner firms (Accenture, TCS, Wipro, etc.) operating contact centres globally. The GTM Strategy & Operations team within gSO acts as the internal consulting and programme management layer that helps gTech Ads leadership make strategic decisions, run transformation programmes, and align vendor operations to customer experience goals.


You are not selling ads. You are not doing technical support. You are the strategic programme architect and transformation driver for how Google delivers customer support at scale — through vendors, processes, and data. Think of it as: Internal Strategy Consultant + Programme Director + Vendor Operations Strategist for Google's customer experience engine.

Project P13 — New

CX Vendor Intelligence Platform

An AI-powered dashboard that monitors outsourced customer support vendor performance, detects emerging quality issues before they impact CSAT, and generates executive briefing packs for vendor governance reviews. This directly mirrors the gTech Ads CX challenge: managing multiple vendor partners at scale with data-driven decision-making.

  • Input: Mock vendor performance data — ticket volumes, resolution rates, CSAT scores, escalation rates, handle time by vendor by region
  • AI layer: Claude/GPT-4o anomaly detection on CSAT trends; automated root cause hypothesis generation; predictive vendor capacity alerts
  • Output: Looker Studio dashboard + AI-generated weekly vendor governance briefing
  • Tech stack: Python + Claude API + Streamlit (interactive demo) + Looker Studio (reporting layer)
Architecture & Data Model

Mock Data Structure

Generate synthetic data for 5 vendor partners across 4 regions (APAC, EMEA, Americas, India), 3 support tiers (L1/L2/L3), and 6 months of daily granularity. Metrics: ticket volume, first-contact resolution rate, average handle time, CSAT score, escalation rate, SLA adherence %, agent utilisation rate.

AI Intelligence Layer

Anomaly Detection: Flag when a vendor's CSAT drops >5% from trailing 30-day average. Flag when escalation rate exceeds regional benchmark by >2 standard deviations. Flag when SLA adherence falls below contractual threshold.

Root Cause Hypothesis: When an anomaly triggers, Claude API analyses the surrounding data (handle time trends, ticket volume spikes, agent utilisation changes) and generates a structured hypothesis: "Vendor X's CSAT drop in EMEA correlates with a 23% ticket volume spike without corresponding agent capacity increase — likely a staffing gap, not a quality issue."

Executive Briefing: Weekly auto-generated vendor governance pack: traffic-light status by vendor/region, anomaly summary, action items, and trend visualisations.
