The Core Mission — What This Role Really Is

This is a Chief of Staff to a Senior Director inside Microsoft's Operations & Partner Group (OPG). OPG manages Microsoft's global Delivery Partner ecosystem — the outsourced workforce (companies like Infosys, Wipro, HCL, Accenture, and specialised vendors) that execute contracted services across Microsoft's business. This Business Manager is the person who makes the leadership machine run: translating strategy into executable plans, owning the operating rhythm, tracking budgets, orchestrating cross-functional programmes, and preparing the Senior Director for every high-impact engagement. You don't own a product or a P&L — you own the operating system of leadership execution.

Growth Mindset Framing Guide

Every answer at Microsoft must demonstrate a Growth Mindset signal. Here's how to embed it.

  • For failure questions: Always end with "What I learned" + "What I changed." Never just describe the failure. Example: "I delegated SQL learning → I learned that skill-building can't be outsourced → I'm now completing Mode Analytics and DataCamp to close this gap permanently."
  • For success questions: Include "What I'd do differently next time." Example: "The NPV model succeeded, but I'd have brought a sensitivity analysis to round 1 instead of round 3 — that would have closed two rounds of scrutiny in 5 minutes."
  • For conflict questions: Frame disagreements as "learn-it-all, not know-it-all." Example: "I sought to understand their perspective first before presenting mine. I discovered they had a valid constraint I hadn't considered."
  • Culture signal words to use: "growth mindset," "learn-it-all," "customer obsession," "One Microsoft," "Model-Coach-Care," "diverse and inclusive," "empower every person."
