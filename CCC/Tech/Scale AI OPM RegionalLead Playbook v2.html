S1 · Structural Pattern Recognition & Durable Fix

Maps to: JD bullets 1, 2, 11, 12

SITUATION
In 2023, an internal audit of Sobha's collections operations identified 14 control findings across documentation, approval protocols, and data governance. The findings carried real risk of recurrence — most ops teams would close them for the audit cycle and find the same issues re-opening next year.
TASK
As audit single point of contact, I owned the remediation programme. The bar I set was structural closure, not paper closure — every finding needed a control that made recurrence impossible.
ACTION
Built a structured risk register: owner, root-cause analysis, remediation action, target closure date, evidence criterion. Clustered 14 surface findings into 6 root-cause patterns. Designed structural controls for each pattern, not each finding. Ran weekly governance cadence with team leads. Proactively escalated three blockers to CCO before they hit overdue dates. The risk register became a standing tool, not an audit artifact.
RESULT
97.5% reduction in error rate. All 14 findings closed within window. Zero recurrence in subsequent cycles. The risk register is now the team's continuous controls monitoring framework. Auditability built into the operating model, not bolted on at audit time.

S2 · KPI Improvement Under Pressure

Maps to: JD bullets 1, 2, 4, 6

SITUATION
By December 2025, PR rejection rate at Sobha had reached 49% — nearly half of all collections-related financial requests sent back due to documentation failures. Volume had tripled (~90 → 281/month) while quality collapsed. In support-ops terms: SLA breach on half of all tickets, rework eating capacity.
TASK
Diagnose root causes, design a sustainable documentation quality architecture, and reduce rejection rate without slowing collections velocity. The AED 8.2B annual target depended on payment processing speed.
ACTION
Root cause analysis across all rejection categories. Built a quality scorecard with mandatory pre-submission checklist fields. Implemented daily KPI monitoring with team-level visibility. Introduced a peer review step. Trained the team on closure evidence standards. Built a tracker that flagged any request exceeding the documentation threshold before submission — the safeguard upstream of the rejection, not downstream.
RESULT
PR TAT 20 → 3 days (85% improvement). Rejection rate declined sharply. The documentation quality architecture is now a permanent operating layer.

Project 01 · Headline build · P11 reframed

Regional Operations Health Dashboard

The solution. A single-page dashboard pulling from Zendesk-style ticketing data, internal Scale APIs, and a Postgres warehouse. Real-time KPI tiles for SLA adherence, CSAT trailing 7-day, backlog by age band, escalation rate, FCR. Three personas: Regional Lead view, frontline manager view, individual contributor view. Built with drill-down from region → work type → team → contributor.

The AI layer. Claude API integration auto-generates a weekly regional ops briefing (TL;DR + top 3 anomalies + recommended actions). Embedding-based clustering on escalation case descriptions surfaces recurring patterns the human eye misses. Project 02 · Supporting build · P12 reframed

Escalation Pattern Recurrence Detector

The problem. "Lead retrospectives on operational breakdowns and implement durable, system-level fixes" hides a hard practical question — how do you know what's actually recurring? Most ops teams patch the immediate symptom and forget. Six weeks later the same fire restarts under a different surface description. Pattern detection by spreadsheet is slow and biased toward the loudest cases, not the most frequent ones.

The solution. Tool ingests all closed escalations (Zendesk export). Vector-embeds each escalation summary. Clusters by semantic similarity, not keyword. Flags any cluster with three or more similar incidents in a rolling 60-day window. Auto-tags each cluster with root cause hypothesis, blast radius, recurrence likelihood, suggested durable-fix archetype (process control / SOP rewrite / tooling change / training gap).

The pitch. "This is the structural reliability bullet of the JD turned into a tool. The Restorative strength expressed as software. It compresses what used to take a quarter of retrospectives into a weekly 30-minute review."

Distributed Workforce Quality Scorer

The problem. Scale's substrate is contractor labelers — thousands of them, working asynchronously. Quality control at this scale runs on gold-standard tasks, inter-annotator agreement, and calibration. Most ops teams discover degradation reactively when customer escalations land. By then the cost has been paid.

The solution. A scorer that ingests labeler activity, gold-standard performance, peer-agreement metrics, and SLA adherence. Produces a rolling quality score per labeler with leading indicators (consistency drift, time-per-task drift, IAA drop). Auto-flags labelers whose score is moving in the wrong direction before downstream quality breaks. Includes a SOP-ambiguity dimension: when many labelers diverge on the same task, the SOP is the failure, not the labelers.

The pitch. "This closes the genuine gap on distributed contractor workforce operations and demonstrates I've thought about Scale's actual operating substrate, not just the JD."
