02 / What they actually want

Role Decode — 12 requirement cards

Every line of the JD broken into what it actually means and what it tests for. Read this before interview prep.

Req 01 · GTM planning cycles

"Support the GTM planning process for India, including quarterly and annual cycles, and help prepare inputs for target setting with Finance."

What it means: They want someone who can run an annual operating plan (AOP) in cycles — quarterly recalibration, annual reset. You drive top-down / bottom-up reconciliation between Finance and Sales leadership. Tests for: planning cadence discipline, Finance-Sales translation, comfort with iteration under ambiguity.

Req 02 · Territory & account segmentation

"Assist in territory and account segmentation, keeping assignments accurate and up to date as the business evolves."

What it means: India will start with broad territory definitions (enterprise / mid-market / startup; or vertical-led — BFSI / digital-natives / GovTech). As the team grows, you re-cut. Tests for: structured thinking on customer segmentation, willingness to redo work as the business changes, account hygiene rigour.

Req 03 · Pipeline & forecast reporting

"Build and maintain reporting on region-specific sales metrics, pipeline health, and forecast accuracy."

What it means: Pipeline coverage ratios, deal velocity by stage, forecast-to-actuals variance, stage-conversion rates, win-loss. You build the dashboards that show India leadership and APJ HQ where the business actually is. Tests for: reporting craftsmanship, signal-vs-noise judgement, comfort with leadership scrutiny on numbers.

Req 04 · Market & competitive intel

"Pull together market and competitive data to support regional expansion priorities."

What it means: OpenAI's India footprint, Google Gemini's enterprise traction, India's regulatory posture (DPDP Act), enterprise budget cycles, Indian language model demand. You produce the briefs that inform vertical prioritisation. Tests for: synthesis, secondary-research speed, ability to compress 40 pages into one slide.

Req 05 · Partner & alliance execution

"Support partner and alliance execution, including co-sell tracking, partner pipeline hygiene, and joint planning logistics."

What it means: Anthropic's India partners include Swiggy, Air India, CRED, Razorpay, Karya, Digital Green. Co-sell motions with hyperscalers (AWS, GCP) are likely. You run the partner pipeline, joint QBRs, attribution. Tests for: ecosystem instinct, multi-party logistics, comfort with attribution ambiguity.

Req 06 · Sales data → insights → recommendations

"Dig into sales data to surface trends, risks, and opportunities, and translate findings into clear recommendations for sales leadership."

What it means: Not just reporting — diagnosis. Why is BFSI conversion below target? Which AEs are over-relying on inbound? Where is the pipeline thinning two quarters out? You run the analysis and make the call. Tests for: first-principles analytics, willingness to recommend (not just report), seniority of voice.

Req 07 · Dashboards & recurring reports

"Build dashboards and recurring reports that give the team visibility into what's working and what isn't."

What it means: Hands-on dashboard building — Salesforce reports, Tableau / Looker views, automated weekly / monthly delivery. India sales team and APJ HQ both consume. Tests for: data viz craft, automation discipline, audience awareness (the rep dashboard ≠ the QBR dashboard).

Req 08 · QBR & leadership materials

"Prepare materials for business reviews, QBRs, and leadership updates — pulling the data, building the slides, and helping shape the narrative."

What it means: Quarterly business review packs for Irina Ghose locally and global RevOps / Finance / Sales leadership at HQ. You shape the narrative — what gets emphasised, what gets debated, what the ask is. Tests for: executive storytelling, slide craft, prioritisation under length constraints, political instinct.

Req 09 · Day-to-day operational execution

"Execute day-to-day operational processes including account transitions, CRM updates, opportunity hygiene, and data management."

What it means: Hands-on, in-the-weeds work. Account moves when reps change, opportunity stage hygiene, ensuring CRM reflects reality. This is the operational floor. Tests for: attention to detail, willingness to do unglamorous work, bias toward action.

Req 10 · Salesforce administration

"Help maintain and improve our Salesforce setup, reports, and dashboards for the India team."

What it means: Salesforce admin-adjacent work — custom reports, dashboards, field config, validation rules, light flow / process builder, integration hygiene. Likely not formal admin cert required but hands-on fluency expected. Tests for: Salesforce muscle memory, comfort with platform, ability to debug.

Req 11 · Rep & leader troubleshooting + global RevOps partnership

"Troubleshoot issues for sales reps and leaders, and partner with global RevOps to implement improvements."

What it means: You are the first line of support for India sellers when something breaks — and the bridge to global RevOps (US-based) for upstream fixes. APJ S&O peers (Japan, Korea, Singapore, Australia) are your lateral network. Tests for: service mindset, escalation judgement, cross-timezone communication.

Req 12 · Sales tool adoption + enablement

"Support adoption of sales tools and enablement resources across the India team."

What it means: Adoption of the RevTech stack — Salesforce, Gong / Chorus equivalents, Outreach / Salesloft equivalents, Tableau / Looker, internal Claude tooling for sales (this is a tell — Anthropic almost certainly uses Claude internally for prep, research, draft outreach). Tests for: change management instinct, training delivery, willingness to be a power-user advocate.

What the JD does not say (and you should read for)

No mention of: SQL, Python, or modelling stack specifics. No quota / comp design as required (listed under "strong candidates"). No people management. Implication: this is an individual contributor role, not a manager role — the title "Director" you may see internally is more a senior-IC band than a people-leader band. That matches Anthropic's broader pattern of flat IC bands at high comp. Read the 10–12 YOE bar as "we want someone who has done this work themselves, recently, for a real B2B SaaS sales team."

Gap cards — classified honestly

Per fit-scoring discipline: genuine vs vocabulary gaps named separately. Inflating fit by conflating these is the standard failure mode.

Gap 01 · Genuine10–12 year YOE floor

The gap: JD specifies 10–12 years. You have ~6 post-PGDM (≈8–10 total counting pre-MBA BITS-era roles). Closure plan: Cannot bridge by reframing. Mitigation: lead with scope and quantum, not tenure (AED 12.97B managed beats 10 years at smaller scale). Be honest if asked — "8 years total work experience, 6 post-MBA at increasing scope." Don't argue the gap; argue the substitute proof point.

Gap 02 · GenuineB2B SaaS / enterprise software domain

The gap: Real estate dues collections is downstream of contract signature; SaaS revenue ops is upstream — pipeline, quota, deal velocity, ARR / NRR / GRR mechanics. SaaS unit economics (LTV, CAC, payback), motion design (PLG vs sales-led), pricing & packaging — you have not done these. Closure plan: 3-week intensive: read "From Impossible to Inevitable" + Lenny's Newsletter + RevOps Co-op archives. Build a one-page Anthropic India revenue motion hypothesis (PLG → sales-assisted → enterprise) as a leave-behind interview artifact. This converts a gap into a demonstration of curiosity.

Gap 03 · GenuineAPJ region context

The gap: Zero exposure to Japan / Korea / Singapore / Australia sales motion, partner ecosystems, or regional rev rollup norms. Asian B2B SaaS comes with localised quirks (Japan's procurement cycles, Australia's compliance posture, Korea's reseller model). Closure plan: Read the Anthropic Tokyo office launch posts + scan APJ S&O LinkedIn posts from Salesforce, Atlassian, MongoDB, Snowflake APJ leaders. Frame the gap as "India-first with curiosity to learn the APJ pattern from Tokyo S&O counterparts."

Gap 04 · VocabQuota setting, sales comp design, accelerators

The gap (less than it appears): "Strong candidates" preference, not requirement. You have not designed AE quotas, OTE structures, accelerator curves, or SPIF programmes. Closure plan: 8-hour primer — Xactly resources, SBI quota-setting frameworks, basic OTE structure (50/50, 60/40, 70/30 splits), accelerator design (1× → 1.5× → 2× at threshold). You don't need to have done this — you need to understand it as a peer when global RevOps designs and rolls down.

Gap 05 · VocabSaaS-specific BI tools (Tableau, Looker)

The gap (mostly familiarity): You have not built dashboards in Tableau or Looker. Your reporting muscle is Salesforce-native + Excel + Power BI. Closure plan: Looker Studio you already have (cert track). Tableau Public 10-hour intensive — rebuild one Sobha dashboard in Tableau as a portfolio artifact. Frame in interview: "I've shipped 70+ production dashboards in Salesforce + Power BI; Tableau / Looker is a tool swap, not a capability swap. Here's the Tableau rebuild I did last week."

Gap 06 · VocabSaaS unit economics vocabulary

The gap: ARR, NRR, GRR, magic number, Rule of 40, payback period, CAC ratio. You have lived these mechanics in different vocabulary — NPV, IRR, collection velocity, LTV-equivalent thinking — but cannot say them in the SaaS dialect fluently. Closure plan: 4-hour primer + flashcards. Read Christoph Janz's "5 Ways to Build a $100M Business" + David Skok's SaaS metrics canon. Lock 12 terms cold before any interview.
