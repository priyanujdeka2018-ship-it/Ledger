What This Role Really Is

Stripping away the Google-speak to reveal the actual job

The Plain-English Summary

Google's advertising products — Search Ads, YouTube Ads, Display & Video 360, etc. — are supported by a massive customer support infrastructure. Much of this support is delivered through managed service partners (outsourced vendor firms like Accenture, TCS, Wipro, Concentrix) operating contact centres globally. The Scaled Delivery Manager owns the operational relationship with these vendor partners: ensuring they hit their KPIs, maintaining service quality, managing budgets, and driving AI-first transformation across the vendor ecosystem.

You are not selling ads. You are not doing technical support. You are the operations leader who ensures Google's outsourced customer support machine runs at peak performance — through vendor governance, process optimization, AI automation, and cross-functional stakeholder management.

Think of it as: Head of Vendor Operations + AI Transformation Driver + Cross-Functional Programme Leader for Google's customer support delivery engine.

Architecture & Data Model

Mock Data Structure

Metrics: ticket volume, first-contact resolution rate, average handle time, CSAT score, escalation rate, SLA adherence %, agent utilisation rate, AI-handled ticket %.

AI Intelligence Layer

Anomaly Detection: Flag when a vendor's CSAT drops >5% from trailing 30-day average. Flag when escalation rate exceeds regional benchmark by >2σ. Flag when SLA adherence falls below contractual threshold.

AI Automation Modeler: "If L1 ticket deflection via Gemini reaches 40%, Vendor A's required headcount decreases by 18% while cost-per-resolution drops 22%. Recommended action: renegotiate SOW scope by Q3."
