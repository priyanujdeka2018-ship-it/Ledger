This is a Chief of Staff to the Site Leader of CoreAI India — a 600+ person organisation that ships Microsoft's bleeding-edge AI products: GitHub Copilot, Microsoft Foundry, agentic AI tooling for developers worldwide. The Unstated Requirements (What They Won't Tell You)

1 · CoreAI Domain Fluency — You Must Speak the AI Stack

Unlike a generic OPG Business Manager, this role sits inside the AI division. You don't need to be an engineer, but you must speak fluently about GitHub Copilot (the developer agent), Microsoft Foundry (the agent platform — formerly Azure AI Foundry), Agentic AI primitives (agents, tools, memory, orchestration), Responsible AI framework, and the broader Microsoft AI strategy. The Site Leader speaks to engineers and PMs all day — your credibility hinges on understanding what they're building.

2 · Site-Leader Altitude — 600 People, Not 60

The scope is materially larger than typical Business Manager roles. A 600-person org with 3-city footprint and US dual-geography means decisions cascade through multiple layers. Your synthesis, frameworks, and operating cadences must scale to that altitude. Tactical detail is the noise floor — strategic patterns are the signal.

3 · India ↔ US RoB Synchronisation Is the Hardest Skill

Two time zones, two leadership chains, two priorities. The CoreAI HQ is in Redmond. Most CVP-level decisions happen in PST. The Site Leader's job is to ensure India is heard, integrated, and accountable. Your job is to make that synchronisation operational. Late-night calls, pre-read packaging in time for US morning, cross-time-zone action item closure.

4 · The Pulse Job — Sense Before They Tell You

"Act as the pulse of the organisation" is the JD's most underrated phrase. You hear things in the corridor, in the 1:1s, in the Slack channels that the Site Leader doesn't hear. You synthesise these into pattern recognition: morale shift, attrition risk, pre-burnout signals, cultural fault lines. You bring it forward as a structured insight, not gossip. This is org psychology applied to leadership enablement.

5 · Microsoft Cultural Mastery — Growth Mindset Is Not Optional

Satya Nadella's "Hit Refresh" cultural transformation — Growth Mindset, learn-it-all over know-it-all, customer obsession, One Microsoft, Model-Coach-Care — these are not slogans. They are scoring criteria in every interview round and every performance conversation. CoreAI is at the cultural epicentre because it's the most visible division. You must embody this language.

6 · The "Background in Product Design or Strategy is a Plus" Tell

Read this preferred qualification carefully. CoreAI is a product organisation, and the Site Leader spends most of their time in product reviews. The role wants someone who can sit in a product strategy meeting and contribute meaningfully — not just take notes. Your AI portfolio (P7–P9 ORE Tracker, Readiness Gate Checker, Risk Committee Pack Generator) is the bridge: these are product-shaped artifacts, not just dashboards. Pitch them as "I've designed AI products that solve operational problems" — that closes the gap.

The CoreAI-Specific Edge — Why This Role Is Different

OPG (Operations & Partner Group) is about delivery partner ecosystems. STCI (Strategic Tier 1 Cloud Initiatives) is about cloud strategy. This is CoreAI — the AI engine of Microsoft. Three things make it different: (1) The work product itself is AI tools, so domain fluency matters more; (2) Velocity is exceptional — agentic AI is moving weekly, not quarterly; (3) Scrutiny is high — every CoreAI decision is visible to Satya and Kevin Scott. Microsoft Interview Format — What to Expect

CoreAI Sr BPM interviews follow Microsoft's standard 5-round loop with elevated rigour due to division visibility. Every behavioural question is scored against the Model-Coach-Care leadership framework. Growth Mindset is evaluated explicitly — every failure story must end with what you learned and how you changed. The "As Appropriate" (AA) round — usually round 5 — is where a senior leader makes the final hire/no-hire decision. For CoreAI, the AA is often the Site Leader themselves or their direct peer.

Round 1 · 30 min
Recruiter Screen

Microsoft India Talent Acquisition. Validates basic fit — qualifications, scope, motivation, comp expectations. Light on technical, heavy on narrative. Goal: get a strong handoff to hiring manager.

Hiring Manager (Site Leader's COS today, or proxy)

Deep dive on operational scope at Sobha. Probes RoB, financial governance, cross-functional orchestration. Expect 3 STAR questions and 1 case-style question. Goal: establish operational credibility.

Round 3 · 60 min
Site Leader / Senior Director

The role's principal. Probes leadership altitude, executive presence, strategic mindset, AI-fluency. AI Site Pulse demo lands here. Goal: demonstrate Chief-of-Staff-level strategic synthesis.

Round 4 · 45 min × 2
Cross-Functional Panel

Two interviews back-to-back. Likely: a CoreAI engineering or product peer + an HR/People Strategy partner. Probes collaboration, One Microsoft thinking, cultural fit. Goal: show v-Team fluency and culture alignment.

Round 5 · 45 min
AA (As Appropriate) — Senior Leader · Often CVP-level or Site Leader Peer

The hire/no-hire decision round. Tests executive presence, decision quality under pressure, Growth Mindset, scope ambition. Often a single behavioural deep-dive into a high-stakes decision. Goal: deploy your three assertiveness proof points. Hold strong positions with data.
