Your from-scratch governance architecture, consensus-building across ambiguous multi-stakeholder domains, and 14.9x programme delivery are structural matches for back-office business transformation — but the explicit 10-year financial services preference and Big 4 consulting signal are real friction that the "I built what consultants recommend, in an operating role" framing must overcome.

Employer Context

Oaktree Capital Management — $223B AUM (Dec 2025), credit-focused alternative investment manager founded by Howard Marks. 100% owned by Brookfield Asset Management since Oct 2025. ~1,400 employees across 26 global offices. Four strategies: Credit, Private Equity, Real Assets, Listed Equities.

Business Transformation dept — This is NOT the Information Solutions team (your existing Oaktree pipeline role). Strategy & Ops sits across the entire back office, driving strategic priorities, managing consensus, and owning end-to-end programme delivery. Broader scope, higher seniority bar.

First 90 Days — Listen, Map, Deliver
Structured entry plan for a VP joining the Strategy & Ops team within Business Transformation at a greenfield GCC.

Days 1–30 · Listen & Absorb

01
Meet every stakeholder in the Business Transformation ecosystem — Strategy & Ops peers, Product Management, Technology, Data, Operations, and third-party vendors. Understand reporting lines, decision rights, and informal influence maps.
02
Shadow the 3 highest-visibility active initiatives — understand scope, blockers, governance cadence, and stakeholder dynamics. Don't change anything yet.
03
Audit existing project governance artifacts — project charters, RAID logs, KPI dashboards, status reports. Identify format inconsistencies and reporting gaps.
04
Build a domain immersion plan — read the last 3 Howard Marks memos, the Oaktree annual letter, and the Brookfield integration context. Understand how Credit, PE, Real Assets, and Listed Equities differ operationally.
05
Identify the 3 highest-pain operational bottlenecks — what breaks most often, where do errors originate, which processes have the longest cycle times. Build a quantified impact assessment.
06
Establish 1:1 rhythm with direct reports — understand their development goals, current workload, and blockers. Set expectations on communication cadence and decision escalation.

Days 31–60 · Map & Diagnose

07
For each top-3 bottleneck, produce a current-state process map with quantified impact: break rate, TAT, rework hours, stakeholder handoff count.
08
Identify 2 quick wins and 1 structural redesign — quick wins ship within 60 days; structural redesign gets a 6-month roadmap proposal.
09
Standardise project governance templates — project charter, RAID log, status report, KPI dashboard. Ensure every active initiative uses a consistent format.
10
Present initial findings to Vijay Padmanabhan and hiring manager — not a critique, but a "here's what I see, here's what I recommend, here's what I need" deck. Validate before committing.
11
Propose a stakeholder alignment matrix — map every Business Transformation initiative to its sponsor, RACI, and decision escalation path.

Days 61–90 · Deliver & Establish Rhythm

12
Ship first quick win — ideally a new SOP, a dashboard, or a process simplification that makes a visible pain point disappear. Demonstrate value before proposing structural change.
13
Present the structural redesign proposal with a 6-month roadmap, resource requirements, KPIs, and phased delivery milestones.
14
Establish a recurring governance cadence — weekly initiative status review, monthly exec summary, quarterly strategic alignment check. Own the rhythm, not just the reports.
15
Conduct first direct report development review — tie individual growth plans to initiative delivery. Set 90-day OKRs for each direct report.
16
Build credibility artifact — a single deliverable (process map, KPI dashboard, or governance framework) that becomes the team's reference point. Make it visible to US/HQ stakeholders.
