Why this role exists

Walmart announced in 2023–24 a strategic push to triple India sourcing to $10B annually by 2027. Walmart Global Tech India (Bengaluru, est. 2011, 15,000+ engineers; Chennai second GCC opening; ~9.5L sqft North Bengaluru lease) is the operational backbone of that bet. The Strategy Operations role inside the Sourcing Division is the centralized PMO + analytics + AI-rollout function that lets SBU sourcing leaders (GM, Fashion, Sam's Club) execute fast while preserving standardization, governance, and geopolitical-risk mitigation across the supply chain. It's a strategically important role inside a growing function — not a back-office headcount filler. That's the upside. The downside is that "strategic sourcing" in the JD's "What You'll Bring" list is a domain hard-gate that operating-discipline alone may not clear.

The three things that decide this

Decider 1

How they read "Strategy Operations"

If they read it as transferable PMO + governance + analytics discipline, you're competitive. If they read it as domain-specific sourcing strategy, you're not. Title is ambiguous; recruiter framing will tell. The first-round screen is where this resolves.

Decider 2

Whether the AI-PMO pitch lands

Walmart Global Tech India won the Minsky Award 2024 for Best AI Implementation in a GCC — AI in supply chain is real here, not aspirational. Your 12-project AI portfolio + automated PMO tooling is unusually well-aligned to this specific GCC. Strongest single differentiator.

Decider 3

How they treat 8 yrs vs 10+ stated

JD says "10+ years progressive cross-functional program execution." You're at ~8 yrs (Sobha 2020+ + BITS pre-MBA). If soft-gate, MT→AGM velocity + AED 12.97B P&L authority compensates. If hard-gate, the screen filters you before fit is evaluated. Likely soft-gate at Sr Manager level in India GCCs.

Honest framing

Sequencing: Below AmEx GMNS Dir (88), Oaktree VP (85), gTech SDM (82), GBO Tools (80), Uber Sr Risk (78). Do not displace these. Apply only after AmEx + Oaktree first-round outcomes are visible. If both miss, this enters parallel-track consideration alongside D.E. Shaw DESIS.

Walk-away: ₹42L floor preserved. Target band ₹50–65L (subject to comp-intel triangulation in Section 08). At ₹42L this is the floor, not the target — the role doesn't justify a stretch below floor given fit distance.

Lead one cross-SBU initiative end-to-end with cross-functional leaders — proof of R4. Pick a horizontal where central PMO clearly adds value without stepping on SBU execution ownership.

Deliver the first PMO-owned Quarterly Supplier Scorecard with a measurable quality/turnaround improvement over baseline.
Day 90
Scale & Institutionalize — earn the seat
Present a quantified PMO impact review to sourcing leadership — cycle-time, standardization adoption rate, analytical turnaround. Numbers, not narrative.
Establish the geopolitical-risk mitigation playbook — centralized analytical + governance support framework for supply-chain disruption (R3). Your multi-entity navigation + proactive-escalation instinct, formalized for sourcing.
Institutionalize the AI-augmented PMO tooling — at least 2 automated analytics workflows live and adopted, redirecting team time from compilation to judgment.
