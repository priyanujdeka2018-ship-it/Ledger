The Role, Decoded

01 / What this actually is

Mastercard Economic Intelligence — The Business

Mastercard Economic Intelligence (MEI) is the data products arm of Mastercard. It produces economic research, spend analytics, and forecasting tools for enterprise clients using anonymised, aggregated transaction data from Mastercard's global payments network. The crown jewel product is SpendingPulse™ — near-real-time retail spend measurement. The Mastercard Economics Institute publishes macro/micro insights, economic outlooks, and sector reports. Clients include retailers, financial institutions, governments, and media organisations. The team includes economists, data scientists, visual artists, and storytellers — a multidisciplinary research-meets-product environment.

Role Essence — Translated Into Plain English

"COO of the Product Delivery Machine for MEI."

You don't own the products (economists and PMs do that). You own the operating system that ensures those products get built, published, quality-checked, and delivered to clients on time, every time. You build the planning cadences, the delivery workflows, the client access infrastructure, the quality checkpoints, and the cross-team coordination layer. When a PM wants to focus on strategy, you're the reason they can — because you've made the operational machinery reliable, repeatable, and visible.

This is not a PM role. This is the operational backbone that scales a product organisation without adding chaos.

As the product portfolio expands (more SpendingPulse variants, more Economics Institute publications, more custom advisory), the operational complexity outstrips what PMs can handle alongside strategy. Architecture

Layer 1 — Data Simulation

Layer 4 — Streamlit Dashboard

Interactive views: Pipeline health overview (traffic-light grid), team workload heatmap, SLA trend charts, client satisfaction correlation, AI-generated risk alerts with dismiss/acknowledge workflow, weekly summary export.
