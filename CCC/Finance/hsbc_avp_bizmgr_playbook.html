04 — Stakeholder Decode · Conditional (Triggered: matrixed 3+)

Three power centres, no single boss

The work succeeds on consensus and reliability, not authority. Map each stakeholder's real want, the friction, and your lever. Your High-S/Relator suits this; your Low-D is the thing to manage.

R&C Leadership — your principal
Primary
Wants: reliable MI, no surprises, clean cost/headcount governance, and to look good upward. Friction: low tolerance for late or wrong numbers in front of their own leadership. Your lever: this is your CRO/CCO analog at Sobha — you already anticipate executive questions and pre-empt variances. Run them like you run Adnan and Ashish: weekly cadence, pre-reads, decisions tracked to closure.
Finance — the numbers' integrity owner
Critical partner
Wants: accuracy, on-time submissions into their planning cycle, reconcilable data. Friction: you sit between the function's narrative and Finance's controls — they'll challenge optimistic save claims. Your lever: NPV-grade financial credibility + your 4-layer reconciliation discipline. Speak their language: realisation, run-rate, accruals.
HR — the resourcing-process owner
Critical partner
Wants: headcount approvals to follow workflow; hiring governed, not bypassed. Friction: HR controls the very process you're trying to expedite — you govern with them. Your lever: you built a 42-person hub with HR on hiring, capability and attrition at scale — you've been their partner, not their bottleneck.
The matrixed watch-out. With no formal authority over Finance or HR, you'll need to hold standards — data quality, deadline discipline — by influence. This is exactly your Low-D development area. Lead with your three assertiveness anchors (Interview Kit) when challenged on whether you can drive without a hammer.
