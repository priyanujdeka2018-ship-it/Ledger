The Core Mission — What This Role Really Is

AmEx GMNS is a $1.7 trillion payments network. This Director role is essentially the COO of Risk & Controls for GMNS Product — the person who makes sure that as products are built, changed, and scaled, nothing operationally breaks, no regulator gets surprised, and no risk event goes untracked. They need a disciplined executor who can run governance infrastructure at enterprise scale, translate between Product/Tech and Risk/Audit, and drive accountability across multi-functional teams. This is not a strategy role. It is a high-trust execution and controls ownership role.

🔴

Operational Risk Event (ORE) Owner

Track, escalate, and close risk events. Know what's aged, why it's stuck, who owns it, and drive it to resolution. Think: governance programme manager for a risk portfolio.

📋

Operational Readiness Architect

Build and embed a framework that ensures every product change or launch passes a readiness gate — documentation, approvals, risk review — before going live.

🔗

1LOD ↔ 2LOD Bridge

Be the connective tissue between the business line (first line of defense) and risk oversight / compliance (second line). Prevent friction before it escalates to audit.

📊

Risk Committee Operator

Prepare, run, and follow up on GMNS Product Risk Committee meetings — metrics, materials, action tracking. The VP can't do this alone; this Director makes it happen.

⚙️

Policy & Mandate Champion

When new policies drop, this person owns adoption — intake, review coordination, training, and evidence that teams are complying. The last line before audit finds a gap.

📁

Documentation Governance Owner

Ensure every risk, control, and readiness document is produced, reviewed, approved, and stored correctly. Audit-readiness at all times, not just when auditors arrive.

AmEx ORM Terms You Must Know Cold
TermDefinitionYour Sobha Analogy
ORE (Operational Risk Event) An event — actual or potential — that represents a failure or near-miss in operational processes, people, systems, or external factors Each of the 14 audit findings is an ORE. Your PR rejection spike was a developing ORE you caught before it escalated.
RCSA Risk & Control Self Assessment — structured process for identifying operational risks and evaluating control effectiveness Your risk register process — identifying gaps, rating severity, assigning owners, tracking closure — is an informal RCSA cycle.
1LOD First Line of Defense — the business unit that owns and operates the risk. Responsible for day-to-day risk management. Your collections operations team. You are the 1LOD risk owner.
2LOD Second Line of Defense — risk oversight function (Risk, Compliance, Control Management). Sets standards, monitors 1LOD. The audit and governance function at Sobha that reviews your collections documentation. Your governance reports serve the 2LOD visibility need.
KRI Key Risk Indicator — metric that signals increasing risk before it becomes a loss event PR rejection rate is a KRI. When it hit 49%, it signalled a control deterioration before it became an audit finding.
KCI Key Control Indicator — measures effectiveness of a control PR rejection rate trending downward after your scorecard implementation = a positive KCI movement — control is working.
Operational Readiness The state of being prepared — systems, processes, people, documentation — to execute a change or launch without introducing new risk Your T−25/T+30 framework. Before any collections cycle intensification, readiness criteria must be met.
Past Due Finding An identified control gap or risk event whose remediation target date has passed without closure — highest priority for a Business Operations Director Any of your 14 risk register items that missed their target closure date before you escalated to CCO level.
Closure Readiness The documentation package — evidence, approvals, sign-offs — that confirms a finding has been genuinely remediated vs paper-closed Your audit evidence packages for each of the 14 findings. Not "we fixed it" — "here is the evidence, here is the approval chain, here is the monitoring control that prevents recurrence."
