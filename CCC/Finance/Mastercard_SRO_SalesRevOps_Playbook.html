Mastercard Services — The Business Context

Mastercard Services is the technology, data, and consulting arm of Mastercard — generating ~35% of total revenue. It sells products like fraud detection (Decision Intelligence), loyalty solutions, consulting, and data analytics to banks, merchants, and governments globally. The Sales & Revenue Operations team is the operational backbone ensuring that the sales, order management, billing, and CRM platforms work seamlessly for the go-to-market engine.

Role Essence — Translated Into Plain English

"COO of the Salesforce & Order-to-Cash Platform Support Function."

You lead a team of analysts who run the platform support desk — handling tickets, resolving operational issues, and maintaining system hygiene across Salesforce CRM, CPQ (Configure-Price-Quote), CLM (Contract Lifecycle Management), Order Management, and Billing systems. But you're not just a ticket queue manager. Your real mandate is continuous improvement: analysing ticket trends to find upstream root causes, reducing support demand through process fixes, monitoring SLA performance, and onboarding new products and teams onto the platform.

This is the person who makes the revenue infrastructure reliable, measurable, and continuously improving. When Sales can focus on selling instead of fighting systems, that's because you've made the plumbing work.

Mastercard Cluster-Shared Foundation (8 terms)

Interchange
Fee paid by the merchant's bank (acquirer) to the cardholder's bank (issuer) every time a card transaction occurs. Mastercard sets the rates; they're the primary economics of the network. Typically 1.5–3% of transaction value.
Scheme Rules
Mastercard's binding regulations governing how issuers, acquirers, and merchants interact on the network. Covers everything from settlement timing to dispute resolution. Non-compliance = fines.
Network Economics
Mastercard earns revenue from assessments (per-transaction fees), cross-border fees, and services. They don't lend, don't issue cards, don't bear credit risk — they run the rails and sell data/services.
Processor
The tech company that handles transaction routing, authorisation, and settlement between issuer and acquirer. Examples: FIS, Fiserv, Global Payments. They plug into Mastercard's network.
Acquirer
The bank or financial institution that processes card payments on behalf of the merchant. They provide POS terminals, settle funds, and bear merchant risk.
Issuer
The bank that issues cards to consumers — HDFC, SBI, Chase. They bear credit risk, set cardholder terms, and earn interchange revenue.
BIN
Bank Identification Number — the first 6–8 digits of a card number. Identifies the issuing bank. BIN management is a core platform operations function.
MCC
Merchant Category Code — 4-digit code classifying merchant business type. Drives interchange rates and fraud rules. MCC accuracy is a data quality concern for platform ops.

Role-Specific — Sales & Revenue Operations (10 terms)

CPQ (Configure-Price-Quote)
Salesforce module that automates the quote-generation process. Sales rep selects products → system applies pricing rules, discounts, and approvals → generates accurate quote. Your team will support tickets about configuration errors, pricing exceptions, and approval workflow issues.
CLM (Contract Lifecycle Management)
Software managing contracts from creation through negotiation, approval, execution, and renewal. Common platforms: Conga CLM, Icertis, DocuSign CLM. Tickets often involve: template issues, approval routing errors, renewal notifications, and data sync with CRM.
Order Management
System handling the order-to-fulfilment pipeline: order capture → validation → fulfilment → invoicing → revenue recognition. In Mastercard's context, orders are typically for Services products (analytics, consulting, fraud tools).
Quota Planning
Process of setting sales targets per rep, territory, or segment based on historical performance, market opportunity, and growth strategy. RevOps owns the data and models; Sales leadership owns the decisions.
Pipeline Hygiene
Discipline of keeping CRM data accurate: deal stages, close dates, deal sizes, contact associations. Prerequisites for accurate forecasting. Your team will audit and enforce this through data quality reviews.
Attainment
Percentage of quota achieved by a rep, team, or org. Core compensation and forecasting input. E.g., "APAC attainment was 112% in Q3" means the region exceeded quota by 12%.
OTE (On-Target Earnings)
Total annual compensation if a sales rep achieves 100% of quota. OTE = Base salary + variable at plan. E.g., ₹20L base + ₹10L variable at plan = ₹30L OTE. RevOps supports comp planning calculations.
RevOps (Revenue Operations)
Unified function spanning sales ops, marketing ops, and customer success ops. Owns the revenue tech stack (Salesforce, CPQ, CLM, billing), data integrity, reporting, and process governance. This role is a RevOps function.
ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue)
Normalised annualised value of subscription contracts. Core SaaS metric. For Mastercard Services: some products are recurring subscriptions (fraud tools, data feeds), others are project-based (consulting). ARR applies to the former.
Order-to-Cash (O2C)
The full pipeline from customer order through invoicing to cash collection. Lead → Opportunity → Quote → Contract → Order → Invoice → Payment → Revenue Recognition. The platforms you'll govern (CRM, CPQ, CLM, Billing) sit across this entire lifecycle.
