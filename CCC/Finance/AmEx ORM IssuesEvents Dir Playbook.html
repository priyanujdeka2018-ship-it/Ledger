02 / ROLE DECODE

What this role actually is

Behind the title: a second-line governance Director who owns the truth of how Issues & Events are measured, reported, and quality-assured across a regulated enterprise.

The Operational Risk Management (ORM) function is AmEx's 2nd Line of Defense — it does not run the business; it oversees how the business owns its risk. Within ORM, the Issues & Events team defines and governs how operational issues and loss/near-miss events are managed enterprise-wide. You would lead its Executive Insights & Quality Management arm, reporting to the VP, Issues & Events Program.

In plain terms: you own (a) the executive and governance reporting layer that leadership and regulators see, (b) the integrity of the metrics and methodologies behind that reporting, and (c) the quality-management program that ensures the issue/event process itself meets policy and standard. You are the person who guarantees that when leadership reads a number, the number is true — and that the process producing it would survive an audit.

The three pillars

P1 Executive Insights & Reporting Governance

Timely, accurate executive + governance reporting; trend analysis; reporting transparency for strategic decisions; reporting data management and governance routines. Your map: MIS function (70+ live-data reports, built 0→1) + monthly risk readouts to CRO + MD/CEO NPV defense as executive-altitude communication.

P2 Quality Management Program

Lead the QM program across Issues & Events; ensure adherence to enterprise policy, governance standards, and quality expectations; quality reviews. Your map: QCG — your literal 12-person Quality Control & Governance gate — plus holding the PR documentation standard at 49% rejection under commercial pressure.

P3 Operational Risk Governance & Controls

Risk Appetite monitoring, RCSA alignment, issue-management governance, metric-methodology governance, business process controls, audit-ready artifacts, and partnering on Regulatory Exams + IAG audits. Your map: 14-finding audit register (RCSA-equivalent checks, monthly CRO / quarterly MD-CEO cadence) + audit SPOC role + 3 proactive CCO escalations.

What AmEx is really buying — phrase decode

"Govern metric methodologies & reporting integrity" → You own the definition of truth. When two teams report a metric differently, your methodology is the arbiter. This is a single-point-of-accountability mandate, not a reporting-clerk role.

"2nd Line team … works closely with 1st and 3rd line" → You challenge and assure, you don't execute. Independence is the job. Frame your QCG as exactly this: a function that sat apart from the operators and gated their work.

"Audit-ready governance artifacts" → Zero-defect documentation discipline. In a regulated environment, an incomplete paper trail is a finding. Your audit-SPOC record (every finding owner / RCA / closure criterion / evidence) is the proof.

"Fast-paced, highly matrixed environment" → You will have accountability without direct authority over many partners. Your 77-person cross-geography, multi-stakeholder operating model is the rehearsal.

Vocabulary translation — your language → AmEx ORM language

Learn these cold. Most of your "gaps" disappear the moment you speak in the second column. The unlock sentence: "I've been doing this for six years — I just haven't been calling it RCSA / KRI / 2LoD."
