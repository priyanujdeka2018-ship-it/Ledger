Domain Vocabulary — Alt-Investment Information Solutions

Master these 30 terms in Week 1. You don't need to become a fund accountant — you need to read Howard Marks comfortably and not flinch when an interviewer says "IBOR."

Books of Record (Critical)

  • IBOR (Investment Book of Record) — real-time positions for front office, intra-day
  • ABOR (Accounting Book of Record) — T+1 accounting-grade positions for NAV
  • PBOR (Performance Book of Record) — performance attribution layer
  • NAV — Net Asset Value, calculated per fund/share class, daily or monthly
  • NAV pack — the bundle of reports produced post-NAV strike

Platforms

  • Geneva (SS&C) — dominant alt-inv fund accounting platform
  • Advent APX / Axys — portfolio accounting
  • eFront (BlackRock) — private markets ops platform
  • Dynamo / Backstop — CRM + investor reporting
  • Aladdin — BlackRock's risk/portfolio platform (peer benchmark only)

Data & Reference

  • Security master — golden record of investable instruments
  • Entity master — legal-entity hierarchy (LEIs, subsidiaries, SPVs)
  • Reference data — static data feeding all downstream systems
  • Data lineage — tracing a data point from source to report
  • Golden source — the authoritative system for a given data type

Operational Events

  • Trade break — mismatch between firm's record and counterparty's
  • Reconciliation exception — any unexplained variance post-recon run
  • Capital call — investor money request for a drawdown fund
  • Distribution notice — return of capital / profit to investors
  • Cash-break / stock-break — recon breaks in cash vs positions

Governance & Controls

  • SSAE 18 / SOC 1 / SOC 2 — third-party attestation standards for service orgs
  • Three Lines of Defence — 1LOD (ops), 2LOD (risk/compliance), 3LOD (audit)
  • RCSA — Risk & Control Self-Assessment
  • KRI / KCI — Key Risk / Control Indicators
  • RAID log — Risks, Assumptions, Issues, Dependencies tracker

Oaktree-Specific

  • Credit / PE / Real Assets / Listed Equities — Oaktree's 4 product lines
  • Distressed debt — Oaktree's founding specialty
  • Brookfield — parent since 2019 majority, 100% since Oct 2025
  • Howard Marks memos — firm's public intellectual voice, 20+ years of archive
  • "Mastering the Market Cycle" — Marks' framework; cite if asked about risk philosophy

30-60-90 Day Framework

Days 1–30 · Listen — Meet all Information Solutions stakeholders in Ops, Tech, Data. Shadow key processes end-to-end. Read the existing SOP library. Build a process-inventory spreadsheet. Identify the 3 highest-pain workflows (what breaks most often, where do investor-facing errors originate).

Days 31–60 · Map — For each of the top 3 pain points, produce a current-state process map + quantified impact (break rate, TAT, rework hours). Identify 1-2 quick wins and 1 structural redesign. Validate with Vijay and hiring manager before committing.

Days 61–90 · Deliver — Ship first quick win (ideally a new SOP or a dashboard that makes an existing pain point visible). Propose the structural redesign with a 6-month roadmap. Establish a recurring governance cadence (RAID log, exec review).
