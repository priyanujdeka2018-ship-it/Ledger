S16 · Scaling Revenue Operations 14.9x Over Five Years

JD: "Building and optimising scalable processes to continue Revolut's hyper-growth"
SITUATION
Sobha's collections function was managing AED 867M annually across a single entity with largely manual, spreadsheet-based operations. The UAE property market was entering a historic boom, and the function had to scale in proportion or the company risked severe cash flow deterioration.
TASK
Scale the revenue operations function to handle exponential growth without proportional headcount increases — build systems, not bodies.
ACTION
Built Salesforce CRM from 0→1 across 6 stakeholders; designed 70+ live-data-connected reports; established T-25/T+30 operational rhythm framework; created governance architecture across 4 legal entities; managed 77-person team across Dubai and India.
RESULT
AED 867M → AED 12.97B (14.9x growth). Delinquency reduced 74% (16.7%→4.4%). Same core team handled 14.9x scale through process and technology leverage.

S18 · PCC TAT Transformation: 20 Days → 3 Days at 3x Volume

JD: "Building and optimising scalable processes" + "Setting a timeline to reach goals quickly"
SITUATION
Post-Completion Certificate process averaging 20-day turnaround. Creating revenue leakage, customer complaints, and handover delays — a critical SLA failure in the order-to-cash pipeline.
TASK
Redesign the end-to-end workflow to dramatically reduce cycle time while maintaining quality. No additional headcount.
ACTION
Mapped full process flow. Identified bottlenecks at verification and approval stages. Eliminated redundant steps. Introduced daily TAT dashboards. Rebuilt handover protocols between teams.
RESULT
20 days → 3 days (85% reduction). Monthly volume tripled (~90 to 281). Quality maintained. Client complaints eliminated for this workflow.

S17 · Salesforce 0→1: Building Core Infrastructure from Scratch

JD: "Leading and executing core infrastructure projects end to end"
SITUATION
No centralised CRM or MI platform existed for the collections function. Portfolio data lived in fragmented Excel files. As the portfolio scaled past AED 5B, the manual approach became unsustainable.
TASK
Lead the full lifecycle of a CRM implementation — from discovery through deployment — as the business owner (not IT).
ACTION
Ran 6-stakeholder discovery process. Designed field architecture and workflow rules. Led UAT cycles. Built 70+ reports and dashboards as the MI backbone. Iterated based on user feedback across 4 entities.
RESULT
Platform still in production 6 years later. Powers daily operations, risk monitoring, and executive reporting. Became the foundation for all subsequent automation and AI projects.

S23 · 10-Bank Settlement Infrastructure & Vendor Governance

JD: "Managing critical vendor relationships and building performance tracking systems"
SITUATION
Collections operations required relationships with 10 banking partners for settlement, payment processing, and reconciliation — managing AED 442M in daily flows. Each bank had different protocols, SLAs, and escalation paths.
TASK
Build and maintain a systematic vendor governance framework across all banking partners — ensuring SLA compliance, reconciliation accuracy, and relationship health.
ACTION
Established structured reconciliation protocols per bank. Built performance tracking dashboards. Created escalation matrices. Negotiated terms on 12 mortgage MOU initiatives with bank partners.
RESULT
AED 442M daily settlement operating smoothly across 10 banks. Systematic reconciliation reduced exceptions. Mortgage MOU programme unlocked additional revenue channels through bank partnerships.

10 / Eyes open
Risk & Red Flags
Conditional trigger: seniority step-down + comp risk + overqualification signal. All 3 fired.

⚠ Risk 1: Seniority Regression

The JD asks for "2+ years of work experience." You have 6+. This is a mid-level IC role — no team management mentioned, no people leadership required. Moving from AGM (VP-adjacent) with 77 reports to an IC S&O Manager is a visible step-down on paper. Future employers may question why you took a lower-scope role.

Mitigation: Frame as a deliberate domain pivot: "I chose scope of impact over title — Revolut's revenue operations at $6B scale was a higher-complexity challenge than managing a larger team in a slower-growth industry." Only works if you actually get meaningful project ownership.

⚠ Risk 2: Compensation Below Walk-Away

India P50 (₹30L) is 21% below your AVP-track floor (₹38L). Even P75 (₹43L) barely clears. Pre-IPO equity is illiquid until 2028. You'd be taking a material pay cut for speculative upside.

Mitigation: Negotiate hard for P75+ placement citing 6 years of scaled ops. Request equity grant details in writing before accepting. Walk if cash comp < ₹38L — no exceptions.

⚠ Risk 3: Overqualification Signal

A reviewer seeing "14.9x scale, 77-person team, AED 12.97B portfolio" applying for a "2+ years experience" role may assume you're desperate, not strategic. This can lead to lowball offers or outright rejection ("overqualified").

Mitigation: Downweight team size in the resume. Lead with process impact and technology leverage. Frame as: "I bring pattern recognition from scaled operations to a company that's building its ops infrastructure for the next phase."

⚠ Risk 4: Revolut Culture Fit

Glassdoor comp satisfaction for India S&O Managers: 3.4/5 (13% below global Revolut average). Revolut's culture is famously intense — long hours, high accountability, fast iteration. Your DISC profile (High-C, High-S) suggests you thrive in structured environments with clear parameters. Revolut's chaos-as-feature culture may create friction.

Mitigation: Ask about team cadence in interviews: sprint length, decision-making speed, escalation norms. If the answers suggest unpredictable chaos rather than structured intensity, this is a mismatch.

Net Risk Assessment

Aggregate risk: HIGH. Three of four risks are structural (seniority, comp, culture fit) and cannot be fully mitigated. Pursue only if: (a) comp clears ₹38L+ in cash, (b) the role is internally leveled higher than the JD implies, and (c) you receive meaningful equity. If any of these conditions fail, redirect effort to higher-fit pipeline roles.
