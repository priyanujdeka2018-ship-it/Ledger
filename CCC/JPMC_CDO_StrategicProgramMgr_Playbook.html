JPMC CDO-Specific Terms You Must Know

Firmwide CDO

The central Chief Data Office that sets data management standards, governance frameworks, and modernisation strategy across all Lines of Business. Teresa Heitsenrether leads. Mark Birkhead is the Firmwide CDO.

Use: "I would be driving firmwide data governance programme execution — ensuring consistent standards adoption across LOBs."
Line of Business (LOB)

JPMC's major divisions: CIB (Corporate & Investment Bank), CCB (Consumer & Community Banking), AWM (Asset & Wealth Management), CB (Commercial Banking), Corporate Functions. Each has its own LOB CDO.

Use: "I managed data governance across 4 legal entities — analogous to cross-LOB programme execution."
BCBS 239

Basel Committee on Banking Supervision — Principles for effective risk data aggregation and risk reporting. The regulatory driver behind most bank data governance programmes. 14 principles covering governance, architecture, accuracy, completeness, timeliness, adaptability.

Use: "I understand that data governance programmes at global banks are regulatory-driven — BCBS 239 principles set the standard for data quality, lineage, and reporting."
Data Lineage

The end-to-end lifecycle of data: where it originates, how it transforms, where it's consumed. Critical for regulatory reporting — regulators want to trace any reported number back to its source.

Use: "My MI report catalogue documented data flow from source systems through transformation to executive dashboards — that's data lineage governance."
Critical Data Elements (CDEs)

The specific data fields that drive critical business and regulatory processes. CDO programmes often focus on ensuring CDEs have documented ownership, quality standards, and lineage.

Use: "I identified and governed the critical data elements across our portfolio — the fields that drive executive decisions and regulatory reporting."
Data Classification

Categorising data by sensitivity (public, internal, confidential, restricted) and by business criticality. Drives access controls, retention policies, and handling procedures.

Use: "Our 4-entity governance model included data classification — different sensitivity levels for customer financial data across legal entities."
Data Stewardship

The practice of assigning business owners to data domains. Data stewards are accountable for data quality, definitions, and governance within their domain.

Use: "I embedded data stewardship by assigning report ownership to each sub-function lead — they owned data quality within their domain."
Operating Model
